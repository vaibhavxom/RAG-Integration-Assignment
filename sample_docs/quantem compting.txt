Quantum computing is an emerging field of technology that leverages the principles of quantum mechanics to perform computations far beyond the capability of classical computers. Unlike traditional computers that use bits (0 or 1), quantum computers use qubits, which can exist in multiple states simultaneously due to a property called superposition.

Another key principle is entanglement, where qubits become linked and the state of one can depend on the state of another, even over long distances. These properties allow quantum computers to solve certain types of problems much faster than classical machines.
